Problems

in Existing Marketplace

The challenges faced by individuals in finding the right career or securing employment often stem from a variety of factors.
Addressing these issues requires collaboration between industries and educational institutions to bridge the gap. Here
are some common problems:-


Mismatch of Skills



Problem: Graduates may lack the specific skills required by industries, leading to a gap between what is taught in educational institutions and what is needed in the workforce.

Rapid Technological Changes



Problem: Technology evolves rapidly and students may struggle to keep up with the latest industry trends and tools.

Lack of Industry Exposure



Problem: Students may not have sufficient exposure to real-world industry practices, making it challenging for them to understand the practical application of their academic knowledge.

Lack of Career Guidance



Problem: Many students may be unsure about their career paths due to a lack of guidance.

Limited Networking Opportunities



Problem: Networking is crucial for job placement but students may lack opportunities to connect with professionals in their field of interest.

Insufficient Job Market Information



Problem: Students may not be well-informed about the current job market trends and demands.

Limited Soft Skills Development



Problem: Soft skills such as communication, teamwork and problem-solving are often overlooked but they are critical for success in the workplace.
